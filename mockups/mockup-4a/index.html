How It Works

A structured, transparent engagement process from scoping to verified remediation.

1

Scope

We define target assets, testing windows, compliance requirements, and rules of engagement collaboratively. Every engagement starts with a clear, mutually agreed scope document.

2

Test

Expert testers execute manual and tool-assisted testing, simulating real-world attacker techniques against your systems. You receive real-time updates through our secure portal.

3

Report

You receive a detailed report with prioritized findings, evidence, compliance mapping, and precise remediation guidance. Reports are delivered in both executive and technical formats.

4

Remediate

We verify your fixes with targeted retesting and provide an updated attestation letter for your auditors. Unlimited retesting is included within the engagement window.

Frameworks

Built for Compliance

Every assessment maps findings to the controls your auditors care about.

PCI DSS v4.0
Req 6.2 Req 6.3 Req 11.3 Req 11.4 ASV Scans

Complete penetration testing aligned to PCI DSS Requirements 6 and 11. We deliver the evidence and attestation artifacts your QSA needs for compliance validation. Our methodology covers network segmentation testing, application layer assessment, and internal/external vulnerability scans.

SOC 2
CC6.1 CC7.1 CC7.2

Testing mapped to Trust Service Criteria for your Type II audit evidence. We focus on logical access controls, system monitoring, and vulnerability management controls that auditors specifically evaluate during SOC 2 examinations.

HIPAA
§164.312(a) §164.312(e)

Security Rule technical safeguard assessment covering ePHI protection. We evaluate access controls, audit controls, integrity controls, and transmission security to ensure your systems meet the requirements for protecting electronic protected health information.

NIST 800-53
CA-8 RA-5 SI-3

Control testing for federal and regulated environments. Our assessments align to NIST 800-53 security assessment and authorization controls, vulnerability scanning requirements, and malicious code protection standards required for FedRAMP and government systems.

ISO 27001
A.12.6 A.14.2 A.18.2

Annex A control validation for ISMS certification support. We assess technical vulnerability management, secure development practices, and compliance with security policies to support your ISO 27001 certification and ongoing surveillance audits.
